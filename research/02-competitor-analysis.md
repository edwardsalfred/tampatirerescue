## Patterns of the Top 10%

After scoring 5 sites, the following patterns separate the strong from the weak.

### Pattern 1 — Phone number lives EVERYWHERE
Every above-average site has the phone number visible in nav, hero, and footer — and at least once as a sticky mobile bar. The weak sites bury it in a contact form. **Win:** sticky `tel:` CTA on mobile, animated dial-pulse on the icon.

### Pattern 2 — Pricing transparency is a moat
Only 2 of 5 sites publish any prices. LP Mobile Tire's "$125 flat rate, $1.50/mile" is the single most differentiated element on any tire site we reviewed — it builds instant trust because every other competitor refuses to list rates. **Win:** publish honest baseline pricing on the Services page.

### Pattern 3 — Real testimonials beat fake stars
Mobile Tire Co's named, specific testimonials beat any 5-star-icon generator. The current Tampa Tire Rescue site has one ("Sara T.") — barely enough. **Win:** 4–6 first-name testimonials with specific scenarios, plus a Google reviews link.

### Pattern 4 — Response-time commitments are rare and valuable
Only Tampa Tire Rescue's *current* site claims a specific time ("within 20 minutes"). Every other competitor says "fast" or "quick." **Win:** lead with the 20-minute promise — make it a giant headline number on the site.

### Pattern 5 — Service-area lists with city pages are SEO gold
Bay Breeze has dedicated cities-served pages. Tampa Tire Rescue lists 14 cities in plain text but has no per-city pages. That's 14 missed long-tail keywords (e.g., "mobile tire repair Brandon FL"). **Win:** lightweight per-city sections (or pages) with the same content scaffold.

### Pattern 6 — None of them have ambition with design
Every Tampa-market competitor uses a template (Hostinger, WordPress, Squarespace). Zero scroll animations. Zero parallax. Zero modern type. A premium scroll-animated build immediately reads as "the most legitimate option in the city" — even before the visitor reads a word.

## SEO Landscape

**Primary keywords competitors target:**
- "mobile tire repair Tampa"
- "24/7 tire repair Tampa"
- "roadside assistance Tampa"
- "flat tire fix Tampa"

**Underserved long-tail (rebuild opportunity):**
- "mobile tire repair [city]" — Brandon, Riverview, Wesley Chapel, Clearwater, etc.
- "semi truck tire repair Tampa"
- "24 hour tire shop near me"
- "used tires installed Tampa"
- "rim repair Tampa"

**Technical SEO gaps in current Tampa Tire Rescue site:**
- Single-page architecture limits indexable surface area.
- No schema markup (`LocalBusiness`, `Service`, `FAQPage`).
- No `sitemap.xml`.
- Image filenames are CDN hashes, not descriptive.

**Rebuild plan:** Multi-page architecture with H1-targeted city + service pages, full schema, descriptive image filenames, proper sitemap.

## What to AVOID (mistakes seen in competitors)

1. **Carousel heroes** — Bay Breeze uses one; they're a 2014 pattern that hurts conversion.
2. **Stock-only imagery with no authentic photos** — every competitor leans on generic stock; even one real van photo would beat them.
3. **Generic "First Class Services" headlines** — Bay Breeze's hero is meaningless. Lead with the specific promise (20-min response).
4. **Hiding the phone number behind a form** — kills the entire urgency funnel.
5. **No FAQ block** — only LP Mobile Tire has one. Easy SEO win the others miss.
6. **Em-dash overuse in editorial copy** — clients read it as cluttered. Use periods.
